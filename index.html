The History of America's Best Idea

The National Park concept was a revolutionary democratic idea: that the most beautiful and sacred lands should belong to all people, not just the wealthy. In 1872, Yellowstone was established as the first national park in the world.

Key figures in this movement included John Muir, the "Father of the National Parks," whose activism saved the Yosemite Valley, and Theodore Roosevelt, who used his presidency to protect over 230 million acres of public land. In 1916, the National Park Service was officially created to manage these treasures for "future generations."

Today, these parks serve as living museums of nature, preserving everything from the deep canyons of Arizona to the ancient redwoods of California.
