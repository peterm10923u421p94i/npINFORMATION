The Roots of Conservation

The concept of a "National Park" was a revolutionary idea. In the mid-19th century, as the American West was being explored, visionaries realized that unique natural wonders needed protection from commercialism.

Who Founded the Idea?

While many contributed, two names stand tallest:

  • John Muir: Known as the "Father of the National Parks," his writings and activism were instrumental in the creation of Yosemite.
  • Theodore Roosevelt: The "Conservationist President" who signed the Antiquities Act and established 150 national forests and 5 national parks.

In 1872, Yellowstone became the world’s first National Park, and in 1916, President Woodrow Wilson signed the Organic Act, creating the National Park Service (NPS).
