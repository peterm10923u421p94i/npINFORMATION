Field Notes: A History

The Historic Old Faithful Inn, Yellowstone

In 1872, the world changed. President Grant signed a document that declared a massive wilderness "a public park or pleasuring-ground for the benefit and enjoyment of the people." This was Yellowstone, and it sparked a global movement.

Our story is written by pioneers like John Muir, who saw the divine in the granite of Yosemite, and Theodore Roosevelt, who believed conservation was a national duty.
